# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Threadly serves a multi-role e-commerce ecosystem with three primary user types:

1. **Buyers (Normal Users)**: Casual and regular shoppers browsing the storefront, adding items to cart, placing orders, and managing their profiles. They value a smooth, visually engaging shopping experience with clear product information and easy checkout.
2. **Sellers**: Store owners and merchants who register their stores, list and manage products, track inventory, handle orders, and monitor payouts. They need an intuitive dashboard to manage their business efficiently.
3. **Admins**: Platform administrators who oversee users, sellers, products, orders, categories, and payouts. They require powerful data views and management tools to maintain platform health.

## Product Purpose

Threadly is a multi-vendor e-commerce platform that connects buyers with independent sellers. The product's goal is to provide a unified marketplace where sellers can easily set up and manage their stores, while buyers enjoy a diverse and colorful shopping experience. Success means high seller activation, smooth buyer conversion, and low admin overhead.

## Brand Personality

Modern. Colourful. Adventure.

Threadly feels like a vibrant bazaar brought to digital life — energetic, approachable, and full of discovery. The interface should evoke excitement and curiosity, not corporate stiffness. Every interaction should feel light, rewarding, and visually rich.

## Anti-references

- Generic e-commerce templates with identical card grids and standard hero sections.
- Overly serious, corporate UI that feels stiff and unwelcoming.
- Monochromatic or flat designs that suppress the platform's colorful energy.
- Cluttered, overwhelming dashboards with poor information hierarchy.

## Design Principles

1. **Adventure in every pixel**: The interface should feel like a journey of discovery. Use color, motion, and layout to create moments of delight and surprise, not just functional utility.
2. **Clarity over complexity**: Despite the vibrant personality, never sacrifice usability for decoration. Every element must serve a purpose; every flow must feel effortless.
3. **Empower the seller, delight the buyer**: The seller dashboard should feel like a powerful command center, while the storefront should feel like a curated gallery. Tailor the experience to the role without losing the brand's unified energy.
4. **Responsive by default, adaptive by design**: The experience should be seamless across devices, but also context-aware — accelerating workflows on desktop and simplifying actions on mobile.
5. **Accessibility is not optional**: Ensure readable contrast, keyboard navigability, and support for reduced motion preferences so the adventure is inclusive to all users.
